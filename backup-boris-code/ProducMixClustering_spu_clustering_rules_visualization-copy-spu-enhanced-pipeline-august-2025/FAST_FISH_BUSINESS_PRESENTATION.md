## Recommendation Logic Explained

### How We Calculate Recommendations

#### 1. **Performance Scoring**
Each product in each store gets a performance score based on:
- **Sales Velocity:** How quickly products sell
- **Profit Margin:** Contribution to bottom line
- **Inventory Turnover:** Efficiency of space utilization
- **Customer Demand:** Evidence of unmet demand

#### 2. **Opportunity Identification**
We identify specific opportunities:

**Example 1: Expansion Opportunity**
```
Store Group: Store Group 1
Product: Summer Men's Front-store T-shirts
Current: 166 SKUs
Recommendation: Increase to 169 SKUs (+3)
Logic: High performance (¥3,358 avg sales per SKU) + Large store group 
       (53 stores) + High demand = Can support wider assortment
Expected Result: +¥7,051 additional sales, +3% category sell-through
```

**Example 2: Reallocation Opportunity**
```
Store Group: Store Group 1
Product: Summer Women's Back-store Sun Protection
Current: 26 SKUs
Recommendation: Increase to 29 SKUs (+3)
Logic: Exceptional performance (¥14,879 avg sales per SKU) + 
       Growing seasonal demand + Underutilized category
Expected Result: +¥31,246 additional sales, improved inventory turnover
```

#### 3. **Risk Assessment**
Every recommendation includes risk analysis:
- **High Confidence:** Based on strong historical performance
- **Medium Confidence:** Supported by market trends
- **Low Risk:** Small incremental changes with high probability of success

### 4. **Financial Impact Calculation**

#### Revenue Opportunity Calculation
```
Expected Additional Revenue = (Recommended Increase × Average Sales per SKU × Performance Factor)

Example: 3 additional T-shirt SKUs × ¥3,358 avg sales × 1.1 performance boost = ¥7,051
```

#### ROI Analysis
- **Investment Required:** Cost of additional inventory
- **Expected Return:** Projected additional sales
- **Net Benefit:** Return minus investment
- **Payback Period:** Time to recover investment
